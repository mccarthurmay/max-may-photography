About Me

I am Maximillian May, a Chinese American from Guangzhou, China. I am a junior at Colorado College, majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Art Studio. My undergraduate research focuses on finding a solution for the shortcomings of modern mental health crisis care.

I do photography to preserve the moments of my life that I would otherwise end up forgetting. It helps me hold on to the present just as much as it retains the past.

On top of this, photography is another medium of creativity that allows me to outwardly present the things that I care for in this world. I can express passion, disappointment, empathy, and love through a lens that can tell unspoken stories.

Future Projects

Manila, Philippines

As a part of my Keller Family Venture Grant, I will be taking photographs of the environment, community, and culture in Manila that facilitate the practice of communal mental health care.

Tornio, Finland

I will be exploring a town that fully implemented the mental health crisis care strategy of Open Dialogue, a holistic and personal approach to caring for people who are going through a mental health crisis. This is funded by Colorado College Grants in Writing.

Vienna, Austria

This city holds an incredible history of mental health care, both revolutionary and destructive. I will be focusing on the architecture of care, specifically with the Narrenturm and Otto Wagner's Stenhof Psychiatric Hospital, which have both converted into museums. This is funded by the Edith Kirsch Prize.
